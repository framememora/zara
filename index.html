☽ Two Hearts, One Deen

A Union Written
in the Stars Above

Zara grew up in the quiet lanes of Lucknow, with a love for Urdu poetry, midnight chai, and the old city's timeless charm. She studied literature at Aligarh, carried her grandmother's duas in her heart, and always believed that when the right person came, she would simply know.

Omar was raised in Dubai, the son of a Hyderabadi father and a Lahori mother — a blend of cultures that gave him a warm, unhurried soul. An architect by profession, he finds beauty in structure, symmetry, and the small details most people miss.

Their families have known each other for years through community connections. When their parents sat down together one evening in December, both Zara and Omar had quietly already said yes before anyone asked.

"The Nikah is half of deen — a completion, not just a beginning."

How It All Unfolded

Our Journey to Each Other

✦

Every love story has its own texture. Ours was written in duas, family gatherings, long phone calls between continents, and the quiet certainty that grows when two people are truly meant for each other.

December 2024

The First Rishta

Two families gathered in Lucknow over biryani and sweet sheer khurma. The proposal was formal, respectful, and beautiful. Both sets of parents had prayed istikhara for months before this meeting.

— "And Allah is the best of planners."

🤲
📞

December 2024 — January 2025

Getting to Know Each Other

Chaperoned conversations. Long video calls with family nearby. Omar sent Zara an Urdu poem he'd found in a Dubai bookshop. She sent back one her dadi had handwritten thirty years ago. That's when both families truly smiled.

— "Indeed, with hardship comes ease."

January 2025

The Mangni in Lucknow

The engagement ceremony was held at Zara's family home — a candlelit courtyard filled with roses, relatives, and the sound of old Bollywood ghazals. Omar presented a ring her mother had quietly shown him at a jeweller three weeks earlier. Zara cried. So did her father.

💍
🌙

February 2025

The Mehndi Night

Both families together in Dubai for the first time. A rooftop evening — lanterns, henna, cousins dancing to qawwali remixes. Omar's little niece insisted on applying mehndi to his hand too. It said "Zara" in tiny crooked letters. He's never washed it off faster.

14 March 2025

Qabool Hai — She Said Yes

Before Allah, their families, and everyone they love — Zara and Omar will exchange their vows. Three words, spoken three times, that will change the rest of their lives. Join us to witness this sacred promise.

— "May Allah bless you and shower His blessings upon you."
